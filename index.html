Fake News vs Real News

Learn to spot the difference and become a media literacy expert!

Fake News Characteristics

  • Sensational, clickbait headlines designed to grab attention
  • Lacks credible sources or author information
  • Contains emotional language and bias
  • No publication date or recent fabricated date
  • Poor grammar, spelling errors, and unprofessional formatting
  • Unverifiable claims with no supporting evidence
  • Promotes conspiracy theories or extreme viewpoints
  • Shared primarily on social media without fact-checking
‚úÖ

Real News Characteristics

  • Clear, factual headlines that accurately represent content
  • Named authors with credentials and contact information
  • Balanced reporting with multiple perspectives
  • Clear publication date and timely reporting
  • Professional writing with proper grammar and formatting
  • Multiple credible sources and verifiable information
  • Transparent corrections and retractions when needed
  • Published by established, reputable news organizations

Red Flags to Watch For

Fake: "DOCTORS HATE HIM! This ONE TRICK Will CURE Everything!"
